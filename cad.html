Gyroscope - Bring it around town.

Sept. 2019

It's been about 3 years since I've used CREO Parametric as a 3D modeling tool so I was a little rusty at the beginning of this project.
Gyroscope was a fun way to incorporate revolves, the pattern tool, different constraint connections, and a servo motor analysis. Initially, I
had some trouble with the servo motor analysis; applying a gravity component would make the Gyroscope fall flat on it's side and rotate in
a way that was not physically possible. My mistake was forgetting to define materials for all parts. An animation of the assembly in motion is
coming soon.

Slider - It's electric.

Sept. 2019

The slider game was a fun project to explore some in depth functionality of CREO such as repeating a part instead of creating 9 seperate
tile parts. This project also taught me about defining behaviors of parts by enabaling push upon collision to prevent the tiles from
overlapping when moving them around the base. I used the boolean operation to cut the lighting bolt out of the tiles. The hardest part of
this model was deciding on the picture of the puzzle. How could I not make it the Electric Slide(r)?! This is the first project I've made that
will be brought to life. I'll be using a laser cutter to create this wooden slider game!
